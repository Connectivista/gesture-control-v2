How It Works

GestureControl uses your device's camera to track your hand movements in real time. You don't need to install anything — it runs entirely in your web browser. When you make a recognized gesture, the app instantly sends the corresponding command to YouTube or Spotify.

The whole process happens locally on your device. Your camera feed is never uploaded to any server, stored, or shared. Only you can see it.

STEP 1
Open the app in your browser
No download or installation needed. GestureControl works on any modern browser — Chrome, Edge, Safari, or Firefox.
STEP 2
Allow camera access
Click "Start Camera" and grant camera permission when prompted. Your camera stays private — all processing is done locally.
STEP 3
Connect YouTube or Spotify
Enable YouTube Mode to search and play videos with gestures. Spotify support is coming soon.
STEP 4
Gesture to control
Hold up your hand in front of the camera and make a gesture. The app recognizes your movement and executes the command instantly.

Who Is It For?

GestureControl is designed for anyone who finds themselves unable to use their hands to control music or video — whether temporarily or by habit.

🍳
Cooking
Your hands are covered in dough or oil. Just wave at the camera to skip a track or turn up the volume.
🏋️
Working Out
Mid-set and need to pause? No need to stop, dry off, and find your phone. A quick gesture does the job.
🎨
Creative Work
Painting, sculpting, or crafting? Keep your focus and tools in your hands while still controlling your music.
♿

For people with limited mobility, gesture control can offer a hands-free way to interact with media without touching a device.
🧹

Scrubbing the bathroom or mopping the floor — keep the music going without ever setting down your tools.
👶
Parenting
Holding a baby in one arm and need to change the song? A single gesture from across the room works perfectly.
